B2B Portal: Transform The Way Businesses Pay & Get Paid

Product Design, Front-end Development
9 min read

The B2B portal is an online payments platform, aiming to help businesses pay and get paid easier, make the payment process completely transparent and more secure.

The History: Started From The Finovate

Back in 2016, after acquiring MintChip from the Royal Canadian Mint, nanopay launched the retail portal in 2017 ( the first project I worked on). At the same time, I was also working on the B2B solution - utilizing the MintChip technology to make B2B payment process faster, more transparent and secure.

Because of the tight deadline for the Finovate demo, we took a dirty approach: Based on what we had, we quickly did a brainstorming and drawing workshop with the product team and developers for a minimul viable product.

In Feb 2017 and April 2017, nanopay showcased the B2B platform with integrations with invoicing softwares Freshbooks and Xero.

The Research Phase

After the demo, we decided to redesign the B2B portal. I worked closely with the customer success representative and the product manager in the research phase. The plan looks like:

Based on the interviews with potential users, we created two personas.

Project Goals

The design needs to reflect both user needs and the business goals.Based on the user research, I summarized the project goals as below.

  • Deliver a visually consistent application aligned with the nanopay new branding
  • Reduce users' effort and cost to process B2B payments
  • Empower users with more transparent and friendly payment process
  • Help users to keep track of deadlines

Solutions

Based on research, we came up with 3 solutions to help users solve the pain points with multiple design iterations and validations.

1. One Click Instant Payments

B2B payment lives in a complex context, the volume, people involved and the payment lifecycle made it so different from the P2P and B2C payments. By integrating with existing accounting software and providing limited invoicing capabilities, we managed to allow users to save time and cost on processing B2B payments.

Users can easily view an invoice synced from accounting partners or sent from vendors directly within the B2B system, and pay it with one click in real-time (using digital cash technology).

2. Completely Transparent Payment Flow

Another pain point in B2B payments is the invisibility of a payment process. According to the interviews we conducted, once a wire(cross-border money transfer) or a domestic payment has been set up, it is hard to know the status of the payment, and how long it will take to show up in the vendor's bank account.

We've designed a transparent history auditing section attached to each invoice in the system. Every status change, action, and comment related is recorded. So users will always know when they are getting paid and whether their payments went through.

3. A Dashboard To Remind The Coming Days

The most important task for an accountant or the small business owner is, to never miss a deadline for payment, but try to make the funds remain in the company's account as long as possible. Remembering the key dates is a challenge and people keep sticky notes everywhere to remind themselves. Also, when paying an invoice, the time it costs to arrive has to be taken into consideration.

And this is why we designed a dashboard with all the status of the invoices visible. Users can easily know what they need to do for the coming week. They can also schedule a payment easily to keep them away from the penalty.

Design Process

In the research phrase beforehand, I worked closely with the product manager to identify problems and goals, create personas and map out experience journeys. When coming into the actual fun part, I involved more with the develpment team to ideate, optimize user flows, wireframe and validate design solutions.

Ideation & Sketching

After we gathered user needs and requirements, I held a brainstorming session with a diverse group consisting of developers and sales rep. We had great discussions on how we can solve the problems, lots of ideas and suggestions, and some very aspiring sketches of our product. I have a post about my experience on this workshop.

Optimizing User Flow

Based on the ideas we generated, I decided to move forward and started experimenting with the different users flow to finish the tasks. For regular flows like registration, forgot password, etc., I used general design patterns that have been adopted to save time.We focused on the paying an invoice and creating a bill.

Validation & Iteration

I used sketchings, low-fidelity mockups to validate my design before moving into the pixels. Due to limited resources and time, I tested with inner employees and did multiple rounds of discussion and critique with the PM and developers. Then I moved to the high-fi mockups and presented to the stakeholders before handing off to development.

The UX Impact

As the sole designer, I led the research and design process. It's great to see that my efforts have had an impact on how the teams work together, and greatly grown up the UX influence in a fintech startup.

As the design team of one, I work hard to connect myself and proactively communicate with the rest of the team. Besides presenting design deliverables in our weekly Learn & Share, I also set up design sessions to educate teammates about design-thinking and user-centered design, and involve developers in brainstorming and hands-on drawing workshops.

Gladly, the team started to provide feedback instead of just following the requirements. People started to think, reflect, and look at things in the perspective of users. These are all valuable input to help me get rid of my biases and ego, design in a more inclusive perspective, and make wiser design decisions.

Landing Page

I was responsible for designing and developing the landing page for the B2B portal.

The Design

Similar to designing the landing page of the retail portal, we took the same approach and highlighted the 3 solutions we made to transform B2B payments.

I created illustrations using the B2B mockup elements and animate them to deliver a delightful experience.

The Development

This is my first front-end development project. As a beginner, I spent lots of time learning HTML5 and CSS to make the site responsive; I also used JavaScript to program the image picker, allowing users to view different images using tabs in web and dropdown in mobile.

By doing actual front-end development myself, I got a deeper empathy with developers. I got to know some buzzwords like box-model and specificity, understand how positioning works in CSS, etc. In this way, I was able to design with both user needs and a developer mind.

Two Challenges

Besides the design problem, there were two other major challenges in the design journey: the nanopay rebranding and the change of technology framework.

The fresh and new nanopay branding required me to reinvent the visual experience of B2B platform.

In the middle of the project, the development team decided to switch the framework to FOAM, an open-source modeling framework developed at Google, and hook all the nanopay services to one platform with access control for different clients to access to different services instead of building different portals, to achieve higher performance.

The change of the technology direction challenged me on the scalability and compatibility of my design, as all the services will share the same design structure, similar user flow and experience, with the same look and feel. However, this is also an excellent opportunity, which led to my latest project - nanopay Design System: Accelerating Design and Development.

Launch

In Dec 2017, nanopay launched B2B portal in a private beta-testing with invited partners. We've started to gain more insights once users began to onboard, and are working continuously on improving the user experience.
